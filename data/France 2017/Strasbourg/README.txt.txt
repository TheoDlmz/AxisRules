This dataset was gathered by researcher for the "Voter Autrement" experiment. It was conducted in situ in the voting stations in the
city of Strasbourg.

Reference: "Voter Autrement 2017 for the French Presidential Election - The data of the In Situ Experiments" (see cite.bib)

This dataset contains:

* voters.csv
	- official_vote: Vote of the voter at the election
	- gender: gender of the voter (F=woman, M=man)
	- age: Age range of the voter
	- work: Socio-Professional category of the voter
	- education: Education level of the voter
	- religion: frequency of practice of religious worship
	- socio: Sociability (how much do you go out?)
	- position: On a scale from 0 (left) to 10 (right), where would you put yourself
	- rule_tested: rule tested by the voter
	- vote_is_a_right: would you say that voting is a right? (0/1)
	- vote_is_a_duty: would you say that voting is a duty? (0/1)
	- vote_is_a_chance: would you say that voting is a chance? (0/1)
	- vote_is_a_useful: would you say that voting is useful? (0/1)
	- vote_is_a_useless: would you say that voting is useless? (0/1)
* weights.csv: suggested weights for the voters, based on their official vote at the election
* opinions.csv: Did you like the alternative voting methods?

Votes datasets:
* votes/approval.csv: Approval
* votes/scores_1.csv: Score Voting (0,1,2)
* votes/scores_2.csv: Score Voting (-1,0,1)
* votes/scores_3.csv: Score Voting (0,1,2,3)
* votes/scores_4.csv: Score Voting (-1,0,1,2)
